What is Hypnosis?

To answer the question, "Does hypnosis work?", you must first understand what hypnosis really is. If you are like most people, most of what you know about hypnosis comes from Hollywood. While they may be entertaining, most of what you see on TV, in the movies or read in a novel simply isn't true. Consider a few movies that has a portrayal of your profession. Was that portrayal accurate? Ask anyone who has worked around computers. They know that Hollywood's portrayal of computers is far for accurate. Now take what you know about hypnosis, and set it aside and open yourself as we set aside common misperceptions people have about hypnosis and learn what hypnosis really is.

Hypnosis is the optimal state to make changes in your life. Imagine setting aside that part of you that keeps you from making the changes you want. Set aside that part of you that nags at you, and tells you that things aren't possible. Set aside that part of you that keeps you from accomplishing the things you want. When you do hypnosis with us, you can set that part aside. That part is called the critical factor. It's the part of you that keeps you from changing. It's the part that re-enforces limiting beliefs the part that keeps you stuck. We set that aside when we do hypnosis. We do this by the establishment of acceptable selective thinking. The establishment of acceptable selective thinking simply refers to the process of guiding someone into hypnosis by using a hypnotic induction.

Hypnosis is not mind control. This popular misconception has been popularized via movies and TV. As we discussed above, the hypnotic state is achieved by the establishment of acceptable selective thinking. That means that suggestions being given must be acceptable to you, the client. That means at any time during a hypnosis session, if there is a suggestion given you don't like, you can either simply disregard the suggestion or you may even emerge from hypnosis. Hypnosis is a state of inner focus and control for you, the client. You can begin to feel confident that in hypnosis you have control of what you accept or what you reject.

In hypnosis, you are awake and aware. Hypnosis is not a form of sleep. The thought of hypnosis as sleep can be traced back to Dr. James Braid back in the mid 1800s. James Braid was a physician and surgeon. He observed that eye fatigue, starting at an object to tire the eyes, induced hypnosis. From this he theorized that hypnosis was a sleep like state. Thus in 1843, Dr. Braid coined the term hypnosis. Hypnos is the Greek word for sleep. After later realizing that hypnosis wasn't a form of sleep, he tried to change the name to be more descriptive. He chose the word monodeism, meaning a single focus, but it was too late and the term hypnosis stuck and it has been used ever since.

Hypnosis is not sleep. In hypnosis you are awake and aware. In hypnosis you are aware of all the suggestions being given and you are in control of what you accept and what you reject. When you emerge from a hypnosis session, you will be aware of what happened. The belief that you will not remember anything about your hypnosis session is simply not true.

Hypnosis is not a truth serum. Since you are awake, alert and in control, no one, not even your hypnotist can compel you to reveal anything that you don't want to reveal.

Hypnosis is not relaxation. Hypnotists commonly use relaxation as a doorway to entering the hypnotic state, however being relaxed does not equal being in hypnosis.

All hypnosis is self hypnosis. Hypnosis is a state that you must enter into yourself. Your hypnotist is more like a guide or a coach. In order to enter into the hypnotic state and achieve the results you want, you must have a willingness to follow your hypnotist's instructions. Your hypnotist has the training and experience and knows exactly what to do to help you achieve your goal. However hypnosis is like a dance, it requires the cooperation of both the client and hypnotist, you can't dance very will if your partner is not willing to follow your lead.

Now that you know what hypnosis really is and know the truth about the common misperceptions that people have about hypnosis, you can feel safe and confident that hypnosis is the right choice for you. However hypnosis is not a magic pill. It takes a special type of person to be a good candidate for hypnosis. It takes someone that is committed to accomplishing their goal, it takes someone who is willing to follow the hypnotist's instructions and it takes someone who is ready to make that change. Are you ready to achieve what before you could only dream about accomplishing?
